# Designing Route Guidance Mechanisms to Internalize Various Negative Externalities
Author: Keito Shiraki<br>

## Background
Recent navigation systems provide drivers with shortest-time or shortest-distance routes, but these routes are not always socially desirable. They may increase noise and vibration in residential areas, safety risks, road surface damage, and environmental burdens such as COâ‚‚ emissions. Traditional route guidance systems do not sufficiently account for these externalities.

Meanwhile, advances in sensing and cocommunication technologies have enabled connected cars to collect high-frequency GPS and CAN data, capturing detailed driving behavior and traffic flow conditions. This creates new opportunities to quantify diverse externalities and evaluate routes from a social perspective.
Existing studies have revealed behavioral characteristic in route choice and proposed externality indicators, but they have not fully examined 
(1)the quantitative gap between individual-optimal and socially optimal routes
(2)mechanism design approaches to internalize externalities.
